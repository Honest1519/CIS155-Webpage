What is Visual Kei?

Before discussing Madman's Esprit, it's important to understand what Visual Kei is. Many label VK as "the japanese equivalent of (goth/hair metal/emo/etc)" but this is incorrect, largely because VK is not a genre, but a fashion movement based around music. It began in the 80s, and when it started it was very similar to the Hair Metal we had here in the west. However, it has evolved to become a much different culture with a lot of expressive variety. Even though VK is not a genre, it is still helpful to refer to the bands involved as a Visual Kei Band, as most VK artists tend not to stick to one genre, unlike most other alternative scenes. This particular band is unique, however, as they are a Korean Visual Kei band. Most fans of the band do not describe MME as belonging to one particular subculture of VK, as they are a very unique band (both in the Korean music scene and in the VK scene).

As a musical project, Madman's esprit has a long and storied history. It began as the solo project of lead vocalist, Kyohu, in 2010. For many years it continued this way, and only this year (2024) did Kyuho announce that his newest support members were becoming formal band members. Madman's Esprit started as (of all things) a Black Metal project, with plenty of depressive themes as are often synonamous with the genre. Today, Madman's Esprit borrows from Black Metal, Metalcore, Traditional Korean Singing, and some European Influences to create a genre that Kyuho calls "Blackened Pop". Kyuho once stated in an interview, however, that "both groups (metal and pop) are gonna hate that name, I just like to piss people off".

I have so much to say about why I love Madman's Esprit that I'm almost worried it won't fit here. The Korean Metal Scene is a really small one, and for such a unique and emotionally powerful band to emerge from such a small scene is really just amazing to me. Additionally, the band composes music in 4 different languages (English, Korean, Japanese, and German), which is just insane. Kyuho often speaks out about K-Pop and VK bands tendency for parasocial relationships, and he has done a wonderful job as an artist seperating him as a person from his art form. While plenty of MME's songs are heavy on the subject matter, the disconnected nature of his art leads you to form your own interpretations in just the best way. And their sound- you'll have to hear it in the links I'll put in the history section. There's no band that sounds quite like them.

Madman's Esprit largely serves as Kyuho's creative outlet, and he has dealt with intense mental illness for some time. Not all of their music is negative, but it does get pretty dark at times. If you decide to seek them out, know that some of their music videos are much more explicit than what I can discuss here.

My personal favorite of their album arts, "자해와 해방" (translates roughly to "Harming oneself, Liberating oneself")

Early History (Black Metal, Solo)

The early history of MME is fascinating to me. Because they are a Korean band (and one that remains pretty hush-hush about their personal lives), it's not easy to figure out how the band started. This lack of information makes the band all the more fascinating to me. Essentially, MME started as a very convential Black Metal project, with shrieking vocals, low quality audio- the works. Because Kyuho (at the time, the sole member of the project) spoke four languages, and the music reflected that, it started to gain fans in more than just the Korean music sphere. It helps that MME largely existed on Youtube, a platform where global music like this florishes. While early MME is very intriguing and enjoyable to me- it's not everyone's cup of tea, especially if you're not familiar with the black metal sound.
